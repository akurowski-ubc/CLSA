Users can interpret values as the percentage of green vegetation within each 30 m by 30 m pixel, but vegetative greenness fraction values are an indication of both the area coverage and intensity (i.e., strength of spectral signal) of all green vegetation within a 30 m by 30 m pixel. For instance, built environment factors (e.g. housing, asphalt) as well as tree canopy are accounted for in analysis, and therefore the presence of roads and buildings underneath canopy can yield lower greenness values. Water pixels were masked out using the Statistics Canada 2016 census water boundary files. CANUE staff assigned values to postal codes (1984-2016) as annual metrics and by calculating the mean and maximum of pixel level greenness within buffers of 100, 250, 500, and 1000 m. https://www.canuedata.ca/metadata.php
